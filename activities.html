What we do

Find your sport

Six ways to get active, get competitive or simply get together — every one adapted so that any level of sight is no barrier to taking part.

New to all this? Don't worry. Every activity has trained guides, the right adapted equipment, and people whose only job is to make sure you have a great time. Everyone is welcome — just come along.

None of it would be possible without our selfless volunteers, who give their time so others can play.

Flagship sport

VI Cricket

Our flagship sport and where our journey began. Using an audible ball, tactile boundaries and sighted guides, players can bat, field and run between wickets using sound, teamwork and skill.

The Greater Manchester Falcons compete in league cricket, while beginner sessions offer a welcoming introduction for anyone wanting to give it a try.

When
Tuesdays & Saturdays
Where
Astley Bridge Cricket Club & indoor nets (winter)
Who
All sight levels welcome

Paralympic sport

Goalball

A fast-paced Paralympic team sport designed for blind and partially sighted players. Two teams of three throw a bell-filled ball across the court, relying on skill, teamwork and sound to score. With everyone wearing eyeshades, every player competes on a level playing field.

From beginner sessions to competitive squad training, everyone is welcome. We also invite sighted players to join in, experience the game and help challenge our teams on court.

When
Thursdays
Where
Indoor sports hall, central Manchester
Who
Everyone, with or without sight — eyeshades worn by all players

Football

Small-sided football for blind and partially sighted players, using a sound ball and guides who call out positions and support play. Fast, fun and welcoming, this brand-new team is open to everyone, whatever your experience.

Summer sport

Beep Baseball

A unique and exciting team sport where the ball beeps, bases buzz and every play brings the team together. Fun, social and full of energy, it's a sport that's just as enjoyable to play as it is to support.

Join our summer sessions and help us build towards friendly fixtures.

When
Sundays (summer)
Where
Playing fields, Heaton Park
Who
All sight levels; sighted volunteers pitch & spot

Social & active

Guided Walks

Gentle, sociable group walks through Greater Manchester parks, canals and countryside. Trained sighted guides walk with anyone who would like support, and the pace is always set by the group.

Monthly walks of 2–4 miles, finishing with a cuppa. Family and friends welcome.

When
First Sunday of the month
Where
Rotating locations — parks & waterways
Who
Everyone, all paces; guides provided
