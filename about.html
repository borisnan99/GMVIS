Who we are

More than a team. A community.

From a small group who refused to let sight loss stand in the way of success, to a thriving club running sport and socials every week across Greater Manchester — and welcoming everyone with open arms.

Our story

Born from a belief that sight loss should never hold anyone back

It started in 2019, when some of us were playing for other teams and saw for ourselves what sport could do — the confidence, the fitness, the friendships. We wanted that for everyone who had been told a sport "wasn't for them".

So in 2023 we came together and built a team of our own, with one aim: to remove the barriers that keep visually impaired people on the sidelines. In 2024 we joined the leagues and set up our cricket team, and the Greater Manchester Falcons proudly carried our colours into competition. Volunteers gave their time, friends brought friends, and the club grew.

We are run by and for visually impaired people. Every decision we make starts with one question: does this help more people take part?

Our team

The Greater Manchester Falcons

When we compete, we fly as the Falcons. The gold falcon is worn with pride by our cricketers, goalballers and footballers — a symbol of everything this community has built together.

Mission & vision

Our mission

To remove every barrier — practical, social or attitudinal — that stops blind and partially sighted people in Greater Manchester from enjoying sport and community life.

Our vision

A region where visual impairment is never a reason to sit on the sidelines, and where every newcomer finds a teammate, a guide and a friend on day one.

Our journey

How we got here

  1. 2019

    Where it began

    Some of us start playing for other teams and see first-hand what sport does for confidence, fitness and friendship.

  2. 2023

    Coming together

    We come together and form our own team — determined to remove the barriers that keep visually impaired people out of sport.

  3. 2024

    Into the leagues

    We join the leagues and the Greater Manchester Falcons take to the field in competition for the first time.

  4. 2024

    Our cricket team is set up

    The VI cricket team is established, training and playing at Astley Bridge Cricket Club — with trained guides and volunteers at every session.

What we stand for

Three promises behind everything we do

Welcoming

No pressure, no judgement — everyone welcomed with open arms. From competitive league sport to a gentle walk and a cuppa, we'll find a way for you to take part. And if it's something we don't do yet, get in touch anyway.

Inclusive by design

Sessions are adapted for every level of sight, with trained guides, audible equipment and clear communication.

Community first

The sport brings us together, but it's the friendships that bind us — the bonds that keep people coming back, season after season.
