Who we are

More than a team. A community.

From a small group who refused to let sight loss stand in the way of success, to a thriving club running sport and socials every week across Greater Manchester — and welcoming everyone with open arms.

Our story

Creating opportunities beyond sight loss

We believe sight loss should never be a barrier to sport, friendship or opportunity. That's why we created a community where blind and partially sighted people can get active, build confidence and belong.

What began as a shared vision has grown into an inclusive club offering football, cricket and social activities across Greater Manchester. Together with our volunteers, families and supporters, we're creating more opportunities for people with sight loss to thrive.

Our team

The Greater Manchester Falcons

When we compete, we fly as the Falcons. The gold falcon is worn with pride by our cricketers, goalballers and footballers — a symbol of everything this community has built together.

Mission & vision

Our mission

To break down every barrier — practical, social and attitudinal — so blind and partially sighted people across Greater Manchester can enjoy sport, build friendships and belong.

Our vision

A Greater Manchester where sight loss is never a barrier to taking part, and every person finds a teammate, a guide and a friend from day one.

Milestones

Our Journey

  1. 2019

    Where it all began

    Our journey started when several of our members joined existing visually impaired sports teams. Through those experiences, we discovered how sport can transform lives by building confidence, improving wellbeing and creating lasting friendships.

  2. 2023

    Building something together

    Inspired to make sport more accessible closer to home, we came together to create our own club. Our goal was simple: remove barriers and give blind and partially sighted people across Greater Manchester the opportunity to play, belong and thrive.

  3. 2024

    Champions in our first season

    The Greater Manchester Falcons made their debut in the Blind Cricket England and Wales Northern Regional Division. Losing just one match all season, we were crowned league champions in our very first year!

  4. 2024

    Our cricket journey continues

    Our community continued to grow with the launch of our visually impaired cricket team. Based at Astley Bridge Cricket Club, players train and compete with the support of qualified coaches, trained guides and dedicated volunteers.

What we stand for

Three promises behind everything we do

Welcoming

No pressure, no judgement — everyone is welcome. From competitive sport to a gentle walk and a cuppa, we'll find a way for you to take part.

Inclusive by Design

Every session is adapted for people with sight loss, with trained guides, audible equipment and supportive coaching.

Community First

More than sport — a place to make friends, build confidence and belong.
